Kwame Forbes

Hi! I am Ph.D. candidate at UNC-Chapel Hill, studying Bioinformatics and Computational Biology. I work at the intersection of gene expression and RNA biology in the labs of Maria Aleman and Daniel Dominguez, with a focus on alternative splicing and RNA-binding proteins. I started my academic journey at the University of the Virgin Islands (UVI), where I earned an A.S. in Computer Science and a B.S. in Biology with a concentration in Computational Biology. During undergrad, I was a proud RISE scholar and presented my work at ABRCMS in both 2018 and 2019. Beyond research, I have also enjoyed my roles as a TA for genetics and a lab instructor for introductory computer science and chemistry. I am passionate about bridging biology and computation to uncover new insights in human health and disease. Thanks for stopping by!
